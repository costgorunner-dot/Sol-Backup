# 26 Camera Angles & Shot Types - AI Prompts

Source: https://aishotstudio.com/26-camera-angles-shot-types/

---

## SHOT SIZES (Distance from Subject)

---

### 1. Extreme Wide Shot (EWS)
**Prompt:** extreme wide shot, subject appears very small in the distance, vast environment visible

**Use when:** Establishing location, showing isolation, epic landscapes

---

### 2. Wide Shot (WS)
**Prompt:** wide shot, full subject visible within environment, shows context

**Use when:** Establishing scene, showing full body, environmental context

---

### 3. Medium Wide Shot (MWS) / Cowboy Shot
**Prompt:** medium wide shot, subject framed from mid-thigh up

**Use when:** Character introductions, western-style framing, showing some environment

---

### 4. Full Shot (FS)
**Prompt:** full shot, subject's entire body visible from head to toe

**Use when:** Showing costume, full body movement, character stance

---

### 5. Medium Shot (MS)
**Prompt:** medium shot, subject framed from waist up

**Use when:** Conversations, business scenes, showing upper body language

---

### 6. Medium Close-Up (MCU)
**Prompt:** medium close-up, subject framed from chest/shoulders up

**Use when:** Emotional moments, dialogue scenes, showing facial expressions

---

### 7. Close-Up (CU)
**Prompt:** close-up, subject's face fills most of the frame, neck up

**Use when:** Intense emotions, important reactions, intimacy

---

### 8. Extreme Close-Up (ECU)
**Prompt:** extreme close-up, very tight framing on specific detail (eyes, mouth, object)

**Use when:** Extreme intimacy, revealing details, dramatic impact

---

## CAMERA ANGLES (Vertical Position)

---

### 9. Eye Level
**Prompt:** eye level shot, camera at subject's eye height, neutral perspective

**Use when:** Natural viewpoint, equality with subject, standard framing

---

### 10. Low Angle
**Prompt:** low angle shot, camera looks up at subject from below

**Use when:** Making subject appear powerful, dominant, heroic

---

### 11. High Angle
**Prompt:** high angle shot, camera looks down at subject from above

**Use when:** Making subject appear small, weak, vulnerable

---

### 12. Bird's Eye View / Overhead
**Prompt:** bird's eye view, camera directly above, looking straight down

**Use when:** Showing geography, patterns, God's eye perspective

---

### 13. Worm's Eye View
**Prompt:** worm's eye view, camera extremely low, looking sharply upward

**Use when:** Extreme power, dramatic architecture, towering subjects

---

### 14. Dutch Angle / Canted Angle
**Prompt:** dutch angle, camera tilted to create diagonal horizon line

**Use when:** Tension, unease, disorientation, psychological distress

---

## SPECIAL ANGLES & COMPOSITIONS

---

### 15. Over-the-Shoulder Shot (OTS)
**Prompt:** over-the-shoulder shot, camera looks past one person's shoulder at another

**Use when:** Conversations, showing relationships, dialogue scenes

---

### 16. Point of View (POV)
**Prompt:** point of view shot, camera shows what character sees

**Use when:** Immersion, showing character's perspective, identification

---

### 17. Two Shot
**Prompt:** two shot, two subjects in frame together

**Use when:** Showing relationships, conversations, pair dynamics

---

### 18. Three Shot
**Prompt:** three shot, three subjects in frame together

**Use when:** Group dynamics, trios, team shots

---

### 19. Aerial Shot
**Prompt:** aerial shot, camera high above, showing large area from sky

**Use when:** Epic establishing shots, landscapes, geography

---

### 20. Master Shot
**Prompt:** master shot, wide continuous shot showing entire scene action

**Use when:** Coverage, establishing spatial relationships, long takes

---

### 21. Insert Shot
**Prompt:** insert shot, close-up of object, prop, or detail within scene

**Use when:** Drawing attention to specific object, revealing information

---

### 22. Cutaway Shot
**Prompt:** cutaway shot, brief shot of something outside main action

**Use when:** Transitions, showing reactions, adding context

---

### 23. Reaction Shot
**Prompt:** reaction shot, close-up of character's emotional response

**Use when:** Showing impact, building empathy, comedic timing

---

### 24. Cut-In Shot
**Prompt:** cut-in shot, camera moves closer into the action

**Use when:** Building intensity, focusing attention, creating intimacy

---

### 25. Crossing the Line / 180° Rule
**Prompt:** camera stays on one side of imaginary line between subjects

**Use when:** Maintaining spatial continuity, avoiding confusion

---

### 26. Whip Cut / Whip Pan Transition
**Prompt:** whip pan transition, fast camera movement to blur between scenes

**Use when:** Dynamic transitions, energy, time jumps

---

## COMBINING SHOTS & ANGLES

**You can combine for specific effects:**

- **Low angle + wide shot:** Epic hero shot
- **High angle + extreme wide:** God's eye establishing
- **Dutch angle + close-up:** Psychological intensity
- **OTS + medium close-up:** Intimate conversation
- **POV + movement:** Immersive experience

## Notes for LTX-2 / ComfyUI

**Shot Size = Distance:** How much of subject/environment visible
**Camera Angle = Position:** Where camera is in relation to subject

**Combine with:**
- Camera movements (from 42 movements list)
- Subject motion
- Lighting descriptions
- Lens effects (depth of field, bokeh)

**Example combinations:**
- "low angle, slow dolly in, medium shot"
- "bird's eye view, crane down, wide shot"
- "eye level, tracking shot, full shot"
