## APERTURE (f-stop) BASICS

**Aperture = How much light enters the lens**

**Lower f-number = WIDER aperture**
- More light
- Shallow depth of field (blurry background)
- Bokeh effect
- Isolates subject

**Higher f-number = NARROWER aperture**
- Less light
- Deep depth of field (everything sharp)
- Less bokeh
- Shows environment

---

## F-STOP RANGES & EFFECTS

---

### f/1.2 - f/1.8 (Very Wide)
**Prompt:** f/1.4 aperture, extremely shallow depth of field, heavy bokeh, dreamy background blur

**Visual Effect:**
- Eyes sharp, ears soft
- Background melts away
- Creamy bokeh balls
- Low light capable

**Use when:** Portraits, romantic scenes, isolating subject, dream sequences

---

### f/2.0 - f/2.8 (Wide)
**Prompt:** f/2.8 aperture, shallow depth of field, soft background blur, subject isolation

**Visual Effect:**
- Face sharp, background soft
- Pleasant bokeh
- Good low light
- Natural isolation

**Use when:** Character focus, interviews, cinematic portraits, dialogue scenes

---

### f/4.0 - f/5.6 (Medium)
**Prompt:** f/4 aperture, moderate depth of field, some background detail visible, balanced focus

**Visual Effect:**
- Subject sharp
- Background recognizable but soft
- Balanced look
- Most versatile

**Use when:** Environmental portraits, group shots, narrative scenes, general use

---

### f/8.0 - f/11 (Narrow)
**Prompt:** f/8 aperture, deep depth of field, background sharp, environmental detail

**Visual Effect:**
- Most things in focus
- Background clearly visible
- Less bokeh
- Detailed environments

**Use when:** Landscapes, architecture, establishing shots, showing context

---

### f/16 - f/22 (Very Narrow)
**Prompt:** f/16 aperture, everything in sharp focus, maximum depth of field, no bokeh

**Visual Effect:**
- Everything sharp
- No blur
- Hyperreal detail
- Sun stars possible

**Use when:** Wide establishing shots, landscapes, architectural detail, product shots

## LENS FOCAL LENGTHS

---

### Wide Angle (14mm - 35mm)
**Prompt:** 24mm wide angle lens, environmental context, slight distortion at edges

**Effect:**
- Shows environment
- Slight edge distortion
- Expansive feel
- Deep focus naturally

**Use when:** Establishing shots, landscapes, confined spaces, dramatic perspective

---

### Normal (35mm - 70mm)
**Prompt:** 50mm lens, natural perspective, human eye equivalent

**Effect:**
- Natural perspective
- Minimal distortion
- Versatile
- Balanced look

**Use when:** Documentary style, natural scenes, everyday moments

---

### Telephoto (85mm - 200mm)
**Prompt:** 85mm telephoto lens, compressed perspective, flattering portraits, shallow depth

**Effect:**
- Compressed background
- Flattering for faces
- Shallow DoF naturally
- Isolates subject

**Use when:** Portraits, intimate moments, isolating subjects, cinematic close-ups

---

### Super Telephoto (200mm+)
**Prompt:** 200mm lens, extreme compression, very shallow depth of field, background melts away

**Effect:**
- Extreme compression
- Paper-thin focus plane
- Background becomes abstract
- Distant subjects feel close

**Use when:** Sports, wildlife, extreme isolation, surveillance POV

## NOTES

**AI Video Generation Tips:**
- Lower f-numbers (f/1.4-f/2.8) create more cinematic separation
- Combine with lighting descriptions for mood
- Match aperture to emotional tone
- Wide apertures + telephoto = maximum isolation
- Narrow apertures + wide angle = maximum context

**For Orb Night:**
- Star/orb shots: wide angle (24-35mm), medium aperture (f/4-5.6)
- Intimate character moments: telephoto (85mm), wide aperture (f/1.8-2.8)
- Environmental context: normal to wide (35-50mm), moderate aperture (f/4)
